### 3.1 Preprints (6 References)

*   **`priem2022openalex` (Priem, Piwowar, & Orr, 2022)**
    *   *Type:* Preprint (published on Zenodo and arXiv).
    *   *Verification Status:* **Verified** (DOI: `10.5281/zenodo.6936227`).
    *   *Defense Standing:* While technically a preprint, it is the **official publication** for the OpenAlex platform written by its founders. It is widely cited throughout modern informetrics. We can confidently use it, backing its reliability with peer-reviewed empirical audits like Visser et al. (2020) and Scheidsteger & Lindner (2025).
*   **`mcinnes2018umap` (McInnes, Healy, & Melville, 2018)**
    *   *Type:* Preprint (arXiv).
    *   *Verification Status:* **Verified** (arXiv: `1802.03426`).
    *   *Defense Standing:* The primary algorithm paper for UMAP. Although published on arXiv, it has thousands of academic citations and represents the universally accepted mathematical description of UMAP. We use it solely to define UMAP's math for our qualitative-only visualization layer.
*   **`devlin2018bert` (Devlin et al., 2018)**
    *   *Type:* Preprint (arXiv).
    *   *Verification Status:* **Verified** (arXiv: `1810.04805`).
    *   *Defense Standing:* The original BERT paper. Published on arXiv, it is the foundational paper for modern transformer-based language representations. It is globally accepted as a canonical computer science reference.
*   **`mikolov2013word2vec` (Mikolov et al., 2013)**
    *   *Type:* Preprint (arXiv).
    *   *Verification Status:* **Verified** (arXiv: `1301.3781`).
    *   *Defense Standing:* The original Word2Vec paper. Although published on arXiv, it is a highly canonical, historically significant paper in NLP that established dense semantic vector mappings.
*   **`dinu2014hubness` (Dinu, Lazaridou, & Baroni, 2014)**
    *   *Type:* Preprint (arXiv).
    *   *Verification Status:* **Verified** (arXiv: `1412.6568`).
    *   *Defense Standing:* Published on arXiv. It is a highly-cited preprint that demonstrates the emergence of hubness in bilingual lexicon extraction. Relegated strictly to the non-active future work extension section for hubness reduction discussion.
*   **`flexer2016hubness` (Flexer & Feldbauer, 2016)**
    *   *Type:* Preprint (arXiv).
    *   *Verification Status:* **Verified** (arXiv: `1603.04683`).
    *   *Defense Standing:* Published on arXiv. It reviews distance-based hubness reduction methods. Cited strictly within the future work discussion on high-dimensional nearest-neighbor adjustments.
